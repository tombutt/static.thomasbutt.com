About Me

The Start

I started out as a psychology major, with an interest in understanding people better. Realizing that I would quickly get lost in a huge group of undergraduates with similar degrees, I went in search of something fun and challenging. I was led to computer science due to my aptitude for math, but desire to work on something tangible. The creativity of solving problems in code, seemed like a good match. I struggled early on with boredom, in the higher level computer science classes I found a desire to construct elegant solutions to complex problems.

My Business Experience

After an internship focusing on desktop and network support, I quickly learned that my technological passion rested with design and architecture of code. I've worked in the health care, cruise line, telephony, shipping, and other industries as my full time job. I've also enjoyed time developing websites for friend and businesses as well as iOS applications.

Technology Experience

Starting with a Computer Science degree, and some experience supporting computers and networks, I quickly moved to leverage my development knowledge. While in school I wrote in c++, Java, Delphi, and other languages. I moved into being a Delphi consultant very quickly, but technology started moving away from that platform. I moved into .NET and C# services and web services. Both SOAP and RESTful services are second nature at this point. As XCode matured, I found it very rewarding to write apps without the constraints of corporate structure (good and bad at times.) I'm now working with many different areas of AWS to help move my skills and my company forward. I've greatly enjoyed working with the AWS technologies and expect that to be a technological change that becomes a standard in the very near future.

Current Projects

Working on making sites and applications run globally using AWS. This has included Node.js on EC2 that reads from DynamoDB. It's also included using SNS and SQS to distribute information across regions. The goal of this work is to have fast response time, with large amounts of data, stored in multiple global locations. I'm leveraging Java at times for some of the data management processes, but working this as a near-realtime solution that's available globally is the end goal.
